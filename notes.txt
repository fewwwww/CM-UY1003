ESSENTIAL CONCEPTS:
    matter: anything occupies space and has mass
    substance: form of matter has definite composition and distinct property
    state of matter: pure/mixture, solid(condensed phases)/liquid(condensed phases, fluids)/gas(fluids)
    atom/Element/molecule: O/O2/H2O
    compounds: two or more elements

    pure substance: cannot be seperated into simpler substances by chemical process
    mixture: two or more substances(retain distinct identities)
        (1) Homogeneous: milk
        (2) Heterogeneous: not uniform throughout

    property of matter: (1) intensive: independent(color)
                        (2) extensive: volume/mass

MOLECULAR:
    intermolecular force(van der Waals force): F between molecule
        measurement: melting/boiling point
        (polar)dipole-dipole interaction: attractive forces act between polar molecules(CH3CN)
            hydrogen bond: H to N, O, F
        (nonpolar)dispersion force: instantaneous dipole induced in atom/molecule
            polarizability: increase with more electron, more diffuse electron cloud
        ion-dipole interaction: approach, significant
    intramolecular force: hold atom in molecule, stronger than intermolecular

    surface tension: stretch the surface of liquid, strong intermolecular force
    capillary action: cohesion(attraction between like molecules), adhesion(unlike)

    viscosity: fluid's resistance to flow, higher slower flow
    hess law: changeHsub = changeHfus + changeHvap
    dynamic equilibrium: condensation rate = evaporation rate
    molar heat of vaporization: ln P = - changeHvap/RT(K) + C
    ice less dense than water
    vapor pressure: intermolecular force, high at room temperatrue said to be volatile

    vapor pressure: increase with temperature
      ln P1 - ln P2 = changeHvap/R (1/T2 - 1/T1)
      higher boiling point, lower vapor pressure

    Solid:
      crystalline: lattice structure
      crystal types:
        ionic: NaCl; covalent(covalent boind): C, SiO2; molecular(nonpolar): H2O,P4,CO2,CH4,Ar; metallic: Fe
    
      amorphous: lack a regular three-dimensional arrangement of atoms
        glass
    
    phase change: 
      criticle point: liquid vapor boundry terminate
      triple pointL three state intersect

      Liquid-Vapor: normal boiling point(1atm)
        exceed Tc and Pc, respectively, is called a supercritical fluid
      Solid-Liquid: fusion/melting
        melting point of a solid = freezing point of a liquid
        Supercooling is a phenomenon in which a liquid can be temporarily cooled to below its freezing point
      
EQUILIBRIUM:
    physical equilibrium: water and ice
    chemical equilibrium: N2O4 and NO2

    Kc = NO2eq/N2O4eq； N2O4 = 2NO2
    K=[C]^c[D]^d/[A]^a[B]^b; aA + bB = cC + dD
      [COCl2]/[CO][Cl2]
    K >> 1, product favored; K << 1, reactant favored

    homogenous equilibrium: all same phase. liquid not included
    Kp = Kc(RT)^(mole of gas product - mole of gas reactant)
    Kp = rightside gas(if no gas, 1)/(Po2)(PH2)^2

    Qc = [NOCl]^2 0 = [M]^2/...
    Qc > Kc, shift to left
    heterogenous equilibrium: different phases
      solid/liquid all = 1
    
    Kc = (productM - reactM)/react1M^2 * react2M
    Kc = 1/Kc' when reaction reversed
    A -> B -> C = Kc' * Kc''

    concentration: 0.8, 0 \n -x, +x \n 0.8-x, x
    direction of reaction: increasing: more mole -> less mole

    Le Chatelier: 
        exothermic reaction
        increase temperature: favor endothermic, concentration product decrease, concentration reactant increase
        increase pressure: favor less moles, concentration product increase
        increase concentration reactant: other reactant decrease, product increase
        add catalyst/add noble gas: no effect on equilibrium

ACID BASE:
    bronsted acid/base: can donate/accept proton
    arrhenius acid/base: produce H3O+/OH- in water
    amphoteric: can behave either bronsted acid or base

    Kw = [H3O+](concentration of H+)[OH-] = 1.0e-14 each 1.0e-7
    [H+] = [OH-] neutral, > acid, < base

    pH = -log[H+] always positive
    pH 7 netural, < acidic, > basic

    strong electronlyte: 100% dissociation NaCl, strong acids/bases
    weak electronlyte: not completely dissociated CH3COOH, weak acids
    pH: 0.018M BaOH2 -> 0.036M 2OH-, then pH

    Ka/b: acid/base ionization constant
      HA + H2O - H3O + A; HA - H + A
      Ka = [H][A]/[HA] = x*x/0.5-x
    percent ionization: [H]equilibrium/[HA]initial * 100%
    KaKb = Kw

    oxidation number of center increase, acid strength increase
